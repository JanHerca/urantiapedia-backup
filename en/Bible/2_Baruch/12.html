OXY = OXYRHYNCHUS GREEK FRAGMENT, from Grenfell and Hunt's Oxyrhynchus Papyri, vol. iii. 3-7, 1903. Verso.

1 [But I will say this as I think.] [OXY: But I will say this as I think, ]

[And I will speak against you, O land, which alt prospering.] [OXY: And speak against you, the land that is prospering.]

2 [The noonday does not always burn.] [OXY: Not always does the noonday burn,]

[Nor do the rays of the sun constantly give light.] [OXY: Nor do the rays of the sun constantly give light.]

3 [Do not expect Land hope that you will always he prosperous and rejoicing.] [OXY: And do not you expect to rejoice,]

[And be not greatly up lifted and boastful.] [OXY: Nor condemn greatly.]

4 [For assuredly in its own season shall the (divine) wrath awake against you.] [OXY: For assuredly in its season shall the (divine) wrath be awakened against you,]

[Which now in long-suffering is held in as it were by reins.] [OXY: Which is now restrained by long-suffering as it were by a rein.]

5 [And when I had said these things, I fasted seven days.] [OXY: And having said these things I fasted seven days.]
