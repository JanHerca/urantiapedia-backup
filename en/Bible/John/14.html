1 Let not your heart be troubled: believe in God, believe also in me. [1] [2] [3]

2 In my Father's house are many mansions; if it were not so, I would have told you; for I go to prepare a place for you. [4] [5] [6] [7]

3 And if I go and prepare a place for you, I come again, and will receive you unto myself; that where I am, [there] ye may be also. [8] [9] [10]

4 And whither I go, ye know the way.

5 Thomas saith unto him, Lord, we know not whither thou goest; how know we the way? [11]

6 Jesus saith unto him, I am the way, and the truth, and the life: no one cometh unto the Father, but by me. [12] [13] [14]

7 If ye had known me, ye would have known my Father also: from henceforth ye know him, and have seen him. [15] [16] [17] [18] [19]

8 Philip saith unto him, Lord, show us the Father, and it sufficeth us. [20]

9 Jesus saith unto him, Have I been so long time with you, and dost thou not know me, Philip? he that hath seen me hath seen the Father; how sayest thou, Show us the Father? [21] [22] [23] [24] [25]

10 Believest thou not that I am in the Father, and the Father in me? the words that I say unto you I speak not from myself: but the Father abiding in me doeth his works. [26] [27]

11 Believe me that I am in the Father, and the Father in me: or else believe me for the very works' sake.

12 Verily, verily, I say unto you, he that believeth on me, the works that I do shall he do also; and greater [works] than these shall he do; because I go unto the Father. [28] [29]

13 And whatsoever ye shall ask in my name, that will I do, that the Father may be glorified in the Son. [30]

14 If ye shall ask anything in my name, that will I do.

15 If ye love me, ye will keep my commandments.

16 And I will pray the Father, and he shall give you another Comforter, that he may be with you for ever, [31] [32] [33] [34]

17 [even] the Spirit of truth: whom the world cannot receive; for it beholdeth him not, neither knoweth him: ye know him; for he abideth with you, and shall be in you.

18 I will not leave you desolate: I come unto you.

19 Yet a little while, and the world beholdeth me no more; but ye behold me: because I live, ye shall live also. [35]

20 In that day ye shall know that I am in my Father, and ye in me, and I in you. [36]

21 He that hath my commandments, and keepeth them, he it is that loveth me: and he that loveth me shall be loved of my Father, and I will love him, and will manifest myself unto him. [37] [38] [39] [40] [41]

22 Judas (not Iscariot) saith unto him, Lord, what is come to pass that thou wilt manifest thyself unto us, and not unto the world? [42]

23 Jesus answered and said unto him, If a man love me, he will keep my word: and my Father will love him, and we will come unto him, and make our abode with him. [43] [44] [45]

24 He that loveth me not keepeth not my words: and the word which ye hear is not mine, but the Father's who sent me.

25 These things have I spoken unto you, while [yet] abiding with you. [46]

26 But the Comforter, [even] the Holy Spirit, whom the Father will send in my name, he shall teach you all things, and bring to your remembrance all that I said unto you. [47]

27 Peace I leave with you; my peace I give unto you: not as the world giveth, give I unto you. Let not your heart be troubled, neither let it be fearful. [48] [49] [50] [51]

28 Ye heard how I said to you, I go away, and I come unto you. If ye loved me, ye would have rejoiced, because I go unto the Father: for the Father is greater than I. [52] [53]

29 And now I have told you before it come to pass, that, when it is come to pass, ye may believe. [54]

30 I will no more speak much with you, for the prince of the world cometh: and he hath nothing in me; [55]

31 but that the world may know that I love the Father, and as the Father gave me commandment, even so I do. Arise, let us go hence.
