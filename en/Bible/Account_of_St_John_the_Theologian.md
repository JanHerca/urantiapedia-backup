## Description

“Account of St. John the Theologian”

Aurelio de Santos Otero says:

«This is one of the Assumptionist apocrypha that has enjoyed the greatest diffusion, particularly in the Byzantine East. In the West, a Latin translation of it published in 1933 by Dom A. Wilmart is preserved. Other Western narratives dependent on him are also preserved, e.g., the code. Ambrosiano L 58, which says verbatim: “Assumptio beatae Virginis Mariae qualiter facta sit, ex quodam libello apocrypho, qui lohanni evangelistae adscribitur, edocemur.” The first modern critic to edit it was Tischendorf, using five different codices.
