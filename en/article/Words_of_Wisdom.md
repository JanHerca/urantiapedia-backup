<p style="text-align:center;">
“Good will is the mightiest practical force in the universe.”<br>
The Talmud<br>
<br>
“The first and best victory is to conquer self.”<br>
Plato<br>
<br>
“In nothing do men more nearly approach the gods<br>
than in doing good to their fellow men.”<br>
Cicero<br>
<br>
“Take care that the divinity within you has a<br>
creditable charge to preside over.”<br>
Marcus Aurelius<br>
<br>
“Perfection is attained by slow degrees; it requires<br>
the hand of time.”<br>
Voltaire<br>
<br>
“Reputation is what men and women think of us;<br>
character is what God and angels know of us.”<br>
Thomas Paine<br>
<br>
“Solitude is as needful to the imagination as society is<br>
wholesome for the character.”<br>
James Russell Lowell<br>
<br>
“Everything happening, great and small, is a parable<br>
whereby God speaks to us, and the art of life<br>
is to get the message.”<br>
Malcolm Muggeridge<br>
<br>
“Even if you travel everywhere you will not find the limits<br>
of the soul, so great is its nature.”<br>
Heraclitus<br>
<br>
“Discovering the Father and Creator of the universe<br>
is a supremely dificult task; and having found<br>
Him it would be impossible to tell<br>
anyone about Him.”<br>
Plato<br>
</p>
