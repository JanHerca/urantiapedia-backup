<p style="text-align:center;">
When I shed the suit of worry<br>
That I carry on me day and night,<br>
When I strip away the anxious bands<br>
Of tightly held beliefs,<br>
I stand naked to the gaze of God, and I tremble.<br>
<br>
What happens then is nothing short of cosmic love:<br>
Down from the utmost reaches of my mind<br>
Stream consciousness and light in healing waves of wonder.<br>
Enfolded, embraced within supreme creative being,<br>
I am surrounded by the power of righteous thought.<br>
I behold as I am beheld.<br>
<br>
What I had clutched to me as vital crumbles into dust.<br>
Selfish hopes are whisked to insignificance<br>
By a force at once benign and beautiful<br>
That powers stars and galaxies,<br>
Yet makes me feel beloved,<br>
At home, encouraged.<br>
<br>
The love that forgives, serves. resurrects, remembers,<br>
Showers down on me and on my world of pain and fear.<br>
<br>
Our God makes all things newt in spite of our determined doubt.<br>
If I remove rny tiny self, God is there in radiance and mercy.<br>
<br>
How can I cling to outmoded ways,<br>
When our world is breaking open before astonished eyes?<br>
</p>

Stephen Zendt

San Francisco. 2002
