## I. THE MATING INSTINCT

1. The sex urge is sufficient to bring men and women together for reproduction. [UB 82:1.1](/en/The_Urantia_Book/82#p1_1)
2. Marriage is the ancestor of civilization’s most sublime institution—the home. [UB 84:0.1](/en/The_Urantia_Book/84#p0_1)
3. The purpose of marriage is to insure racial survival—not just personal happiness. [UB 68:2.9](/en/The_Urantia_Book/68#p2_9)
4. The evolution of marriage is the story of sex control by social, civil, and religious restrictions. [UB 82:2.1](/en/The_Urantia_Book/82#p2_1)
5. Women’s low status during Old Testament times reflects the mores of the herdsmen. [UB 84:3.2](/en/The_Urantia_Book/84#p3_2)
6. In primitive times marriage was the price of social distinction—a wife enhanced social standing. [UB 82:3.4](/en/The_Urantia_Book/82#p3_4)

## II. MARRIAGE A SOCIAL INSTITUTION

1. Man’s great danger is the unrestricted multiplication of inferior racial strains. [UB 82:6.11](/en/The_Urantia_Book/82#p6_11)
2. The family is the master civilizer. [UB 82:0.2](/en/The_Urantia_Book/82#p0_2)
3. Marriages are not made in heaven. [UB 83:1.4](/en/The_Urantia_Book/83#p1_4)
4. Marriage is a social instiution embracing self-maintenance, self-perpetuation, and self-gratification. [UB 84:0.3](/en/The_Urantia_Book/84#p0_3)
5. It was the factory, not religion, that emancipated woman. [UB 84:5.7](/en/The_Urantia_Book/84#p5_7)
6. Woman has always been the standard-bearer—the spiritual leader. [UB 84:6.4](/en/The_Urantia_Book/84#p6_4)
7. Marriage has always been closely linked with both property and religion. [UB 82:4.1](/en/The_Urantia_Book/82#p4_1)
8. Marriage is a social program of antagonistic co-operation. [UB 84:6.2](/en/The_Urantia_Book/84#p6_2)

## III. TRUE MONOGAMY

1. There were four sorts of wives under polygamy:
	1. Legal wives.
	2. Wives of affection.
	3. Concubines.
	4. Slave wives. [UB 83:5.5](/en/The_Urantia_Book/83#p5_5)
2. Monogamy has always been the idealistic goal of marriage, and it is the yardstick measuring the advance of civilization. [UB 83:6.7](/en/The_Urantia_Book/83#p6_7)
3. Early woman was not a friend and lover—rather a servant and childbearer. [UB 84:4.3](/en/The_Urantia_Book/84#p4_3)
4. It was a great advance when the wife could own property. [UB 84:4.10](/en/The_Urantia_Book/84#p4_10)
5. But primitive women did not pity themselves. [UB 84:4.11](/en/The_Urantia_Book/84#p4_11)

## IV. FAMILY LIFE

1. Man did not intentionally seize woman’s rights—it was all an unconscious process. [UB 84:5.4](/en/The_Urantia_Book/84#p5_4)
2. Will modern woman be worthy of her newly won dignity and equality? [UB 84:5.10](/en/The_Urantia_Book/84#p5_10)
3. Divorce will persist as long as young people are not properly prepared for marriage. [UB 83:7.8](/en/The_Urantia_Book/83#p7_8)
4. The family-council of the Andites would be helpful today. [UB 84:7.29](/en/The_Urantia_Book/84#p7_29)
5. The differences between men and women persist throughout the ascendant career—even in the Corps of Finality. [UB 84:6.6](/en/The_Urantia_Book/84#p6_6)
