All things work together for good. [1] And evil. [2] Attempted service of goodness no substitute for religious experience. [3] Basing life on highest consciousness of. [4] Cannot come of evil to evildoer. [5] Concept not inherent in physics. [6]

Defined as

Carrying out divine plans. [7] Finite maximum of experience. [8] Fruit of the spirit. [9] Health of the soul. [10] Living, relative, evil-contrasted, always a personal experience. [11] Man’s effort to discern God in spirit. [12] Nearness to divinity. [13] Revelation of Deity, infinite values. [14] Shown in loving ministry of Father, Son, Spirit. [15] Stabilizing. [16] Understandable only in relation to personality. [17] Value-realization of God-consciousness. [18]

Divinity comprehensible as. [19]

Goodness of God

Beneficence to the righteous and the wicked. [20] Beyond man’s comprehension. [21] Found only in personal religious experience. [22] Greatest evidence is indwelling Adjuster. [23] Leads men into repentance. [24]

Indiscriminate kindness causes many social evils. [25] Jesus exalted goodness by commanding performance. [26] Jesus went about doing good. [27] Man’s understanding of goodness only relatively true. [28] More a quest than a possession. [29]

Nature of goodness

Act is potent in proportion to divinity of motivation. [30] All goodness takes origin in Father. [31] Become good through grace. [32] Blesses everything, harms nothing; seeks lowest places. [33] Cannot be divorced from greatness. [34] Destroys evil. [35] Goodness may be derived from time-limited evil. [36] Highest forms of goodness are unconscious. [37] Human likes and dislikes do not determine goodness. [38] No good act ever wholly lost. [39] Understandable by contrast with evil. [40] Unified with beauty, truth, righteousness. [41] When devoid of grace, goodness often repels. [42]

None is good but God. [43] Return good for evil. [44] Sincere pursuit of goodness leads to God. [45]
