Goodness

All things work together for good. [1]All things work together for good. [2]And evil. [3]Attempted service of goodness no substitute for religious experience. [4]Basing life on highest consciousness of. [5]Cannot come of evil to evildoer. [6]Concept not inherent in physics. [7]

Defined as

Carrying out divine plans. [8]Finite maximum of experience. [9]Fruit of the spirit. [10]Health of the soul. [11]Living, relative, evil-contrasted, always a personal experience. [12]Man’s effort to discern God in spirit. [13]Nearness to divinity. [14]Revelation of Deity, infinite values. [15]Shown in loving ministry of Father, Son, Spirit. [16]Stabilizing. [17]Understandable only in relation to personality. [18]Value-realization of God-consciousness. [19]

Goodness of God

Beneficence to the righteous and the wicked. [21]Beyond man’s comprehension. [22]Found only in personal religious experience. [23]Greatest evidence is indwelling Adjuster. [24]Leads men into repentance. [25]

Nature of goodness

Act is potent in proportion to divinity of motivation. [31]All goodness takes origin in Father. [32]Become good through grace. [33]Blesses everything, harms nothing; seeks lowest places. [34]Cannot be divorced from greatness. [35]Destroys evil. [36]Goodness may be derived from time-limited evil. [37]Highest forms of goodness are unconscious. [38]Human likes and dislikes do not determine goodness. [39]No good act ever wholly lost. [40]Understandable by contrast with evil. [41]Unified with beauty, truth, righteousness. [42]When devoid of grace, goodness often repels. [43]
