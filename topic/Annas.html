Annas

See also: UB 190:3.3.

Former high priest; most powerful individual in all Jewry; father-in-law of Caiaphas. [1]Deposed as high priest in A.D. 13. [2]Distant relative of Salome. [3]Spent much time with Jesus in A.D. 22. [4]Jesus called upon A.; was coldly received. [5]“sons of Annas” had bazaars in temple; overthrown. [6]Enmity aroused when Jesus threatened revenues. [7]

Interrogation and judgement

Jesus’ examination by. [8]Reluctant to participate in murder of Jesus. [9]Romans accustomed to dealing with. [10]Told Roman captain to bring Jesus to him. [11]Palace on Olivet near Gethsemane. [12]Disturbed by Jesus’ refusal to answer. [13]Chief steward of Annas struck Jesus in face. [14]Sent Jesus bound to Caiaphas. [15]
