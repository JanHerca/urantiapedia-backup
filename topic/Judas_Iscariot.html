Judas Iscariot

See also: UB 183; UB 139:12; UB 143:3.5; UB 144:1.7; UB 147:2.1; UB 151:2.8; UB 177:4; UB 178:2.6; UB 193:4.

Adversely affected by entry into Jerusalem. [1]Attempted to heal James of Safed’s son. [2]

Betrayal

Apostles’ suspicions. [3]Betrayed Jesus with a kiss. [4]Did not betray Jesus for money. [5]Emotions precipitating betrayal. [6]Feared for himself; asked for 40 soldiers. [7]Judas believed he would receive high honors. [8]

Born in Kerioth; best-educated apostle. [9]Call of. [10]Carried bag in deep pocket. [11]

Causes of downfall

See also: UB 157:7.2-4; UB 193:4.

Failed to love truth; intellectual pride. [12]Influenced by ridicule of Sadducean friends. [13]Isolation, over-individualism. [14]Love grew cold; refused to trust. [15]Met with enemies of Jesus. [16]Nursed grudges; sought revenge for hurt feelings. [17]Pampered and spoiled only son of unwise parents. [18]Refused to confide in fellow apostles. [19]Sought advice and consolation of those indifferent or hostile to the kingdom. [20]Was self-satisfied. [21]Well-intentioned but cowardly and self-centered. [22]

Criticized Nathaniel; rebuked by Jesus. [23]David Zebedee gave funds to. [24]David Zebedee knew of plot to kill Jesus. [25]Deposited funds. [26]Discharged office of treasurer faithfully and efficiently. [27]Disowned by parents over John the Baptist. [28]Favored proclaiming Jesus king; imagined Jesus regretted. [29]Foot-washing proved Jesus not Israel’s deliverer. [30]Great executive, stickler for organization. [31]Handed bag containing 30 pieces of silver. [32]Imagined Jesus feared Jewish leaders. [33]

Jesus

Advised placing utmost confidence in Judas. [34]Did everything possible to save Judas. [35]Judas was faith adventure to. [36]Last words to Judas. [37]Personal salutation to Judas. [38]Warned apostles of impending betrayal. [39]

Mary’s waste of ointment mobilized J.’s hatred. [40]Name eschewed. [41]Only son of wealthy Sadducees, unmarried, living in Jericho, only Judean, selected by Nathaniel; 30 in A.D. 26. [42]Prejudiced against Samaritans. [43]Present at Jesus’ trial before Pilate and Sanhedrin. [44]Pricked about his traitorous conduct. [45]Repented. [46]Resented Jesus not saving John the Baptist. [47]Saw Jesus nailed on cross. [48]Seized seat of honor at Last Supper. [49]Suicide by hanging in valley of Hinnom. [50]Temple guards, centurion held in contempt. [51]Turned over all apostolic funds to David. [52]Wondered how to break off from apostles. [53]
