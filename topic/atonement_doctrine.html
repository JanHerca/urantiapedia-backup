Atonement doctrine

See also: UB 186:5.

Childish scheme of substituting innocent sufferer for guilty offender. [1]Erroneous teaching. [2]Father had nothing to do with Jesus’ torture. [3]Father’s justice not influenced by acts of his creatures. [4]Father’s love not dependent on Sons’ bestowals. [5]God never torn by conflicting attitudes. [6]Grounded in selfishness. [7]

History

Andonic animal sacrifice preserved in. [8]Christ became all-sufficient human sacrifice. [9]Intended to make gospel acceptable to Jews. [10]Mithraic origin. [11]Paul’s teaching original. [12]Philo taught God forgave only when blood shed. [13]Primitives sacrificed to restore relationship with God. [14]

Incompatible with teachings of Jesus. [15]Jesus did not bestow himself to reconcile an angry God. [16]Never necessary to influence Father to be merciful. [17]Philosophic assault upon unity and free-willness of God. [18]Place in evolution of religious observances. [19]Puerile and primitive; affront to God. [20]Ransom paid to purchase man from devil. [21]Sacrifice of innocent Son to appease wrath of offended God. [22]Supplant with concept of divine attunement. [23]
