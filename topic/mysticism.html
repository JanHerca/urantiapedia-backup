Mysticism

See also: UB 100:5.

Be not passive mystics. [1]Christian mysticism bordered on unreality; philosophically akin to pantheism. [2]Factors which induce mystic state. [3]May become technique of reality avoidance. [4]Mystic state characterized by diffusion of consciousness with vivid islands of focal attention. [5]Mystics do not correctly visualize reality. [6]Practical validations. [7]Practice of presence of God is altogether praiseworthy. [8]Product of mortal intellect indistinguishable from that of Adjuster. [9]Reprehensible when leads to isolation or fanaticism. [10]
