Consecration of choice

See also: UB 111:5.

Choice to be like God is superfinite. [1]Consecreated mortal is already a finaliter to God. [2]Creativity of inner life can be haphazard or constructive. [3]

Defined

Affirmation of desire to do Father’s will. [4]Degree of devotion to divine values. [5]Highest consecreation is to do will of God. [6]Identification of self with universe, dedication to service of universe family. [7]Not passive or slavish surrender to Adjuster. [8]Not surrender, but glorification, of will. [9]Passport to divine attainment. [10]Realization of eternity-reality of purpose. [11]Spirit domination of energy phase of existence. [12]Subordination to spiritual urges of Adjuster. [13]

Examples of consecration

Adjuster empowered Amadon’s loyalty. [14]Andon and Fonta’s decision to live with, for each other. [15]John Mark commanded full attention of God by his supreme desire. [16]Van’s loyalty resulted from unquestioning dedication. [17]Widow’s mites. [18]

Father desires only loyalty which is voluntary, wholehearted, and sophistry-proof. [19]Greatest gift to God. [20]Jesus’ faith destroyed every conflicting desire. [21]Mind arena of choice. [22]Mortal’s 1st jubilee. [23]

Parables regarding consecration

Pearl of great price. [24]Treasure in field. [25]

Result of consecration

Brought Adjuster prior to Spirit of Truth. [26]Determines presence of God. [27]Divine stability produced by universe insight. [28]Divine union attained by wholehearted and intelligent conformity to God’s will. [29]Father is destiny of all who wholeheartedly choose his will. [30]Halfhearted devotion will be unavailing. [31]Immediately reacted to by the Supreme. [32]Joins spiritual faith to material decisions; supplies spiritual fulcrum. [33]Leads to assignment of guardian of destiny. [34]Produces consciousness of God; ensures survival. [35]When man gives God all he has, God makes him more than he is. [36]

We are so devoid of courageous decisions, consecrated co-operation. [37]With Father effected through Jesus. [38]Work at what one finds to do with all one’s might. [39]
