Mystery cults

See also: UB 98:4; UB 121:5.

Augustus tried to destroy. [1]Contended with Stoicism. [2]Influenced Christianity. [3]Mithras supplanted Phrygian, Egyptian mysteries. [4]Most popular were Cybele and Attis; Osiris and Isis; Mithras. [5]Nonnational, interracial, personal, and fraternal. [6]Offered hopes of personal salvation; provided diversion and excitement. [7]Originated in Egypt and Levant. [8]Orphic brotherhood was the best. [9]Pertained to some god’s life, death, and rebirth. [10]Plunged Greece into intellectual stagnation, moral depravity, spiritual poverty. [11]Prepared way for Jesus, Christianity. [12]Secret rites were sometimes gruesome. [13]
