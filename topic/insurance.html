Insurance

Among primitives

Children were old-age insurance. [1]Civilization, laws were insurance against violent death. [2]Food storage was insurance against famine and disaster. [3]Ghost cult was insurance against disaster. [4]Industry grew up as insurance against famine. [5]Religion exacted burdensome premiums of fear, superstition, dread, and priest gifts. [6]

Old-age insurance in continental nation. [7]Reserves against risk of incapacity in light and life. [8]Society lessens risk in individuals’ living. [9]Wise investment of earnings against future adversity. [10]
