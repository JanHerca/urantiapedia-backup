The children of Badonan developed a belief in two souls, the breath and the shadow. [1] They were a people fearful of ice and water. [2] They never practiced human sacrifices, but the other Andonites did it. [3]

During a long period of cultural decadence the Foxhall peoples of England and the Badonan tribes northwest of India continued to hold on to some of the traditions of Andon and certain remnants of the culture of Onagar. [4]

850,000 years ago, the superior tribes of Badonán began a war of extermination against their inferior neighbors. This slightly improved the Badonite race and its descendants is what is now known as the Neanderthal race. [5] 500,000 years ago a relentless warfare in northwestern highlands of India left only most intelligent alive. [6] Sangik races born into existence thanks to Badonites. [7]
