Cults were insurance against hazards of bad luck. [1] Early peoples associated ghosts with the sea. [2]

Fear of ghosts

Disciplined and controlled primitives. [3] Drove men into society. [4] Fountainhead of ethics, religion. [5] Prepared men’s minds for “fear of the Lord”. [6] Produced dread of being alone. [7] Savages lived in fear of ghosts. [8] Senseless superstition. [9]

Illusory ghosts environment of primitive man. [10] Moses sought to uproot ghost cults. [11] Most primitive idea of human souls. [12]

Nature and activities of ghosts

Administered justice through medicine men. [13] Angered ghosts caused misfortune, unhappiness. [14] Caused disease by enticing soul out of body. [15] Credited with supernatural powers, but not supernatural intelligence. [16] Enjoyed unlimited rights but no duties. [17] Expected at least one slave wife be buried alive with. [18] Explained the unusual and the inexplicable. [19] Frightened by noise. [20] Good and bad ghosts. [21] Levied continuous tribute for noninterference. [22] Preferred to indwell objects they owned when alive. [23] Punished those who disdained their rules. [24] Took pleasure in discomfort of living. [25]

Placation long preceded coercion and supplication. [26] Rites designed to avoid, expel, or coerce. [27] Savage believed ghosts to be breath minus body. [28] Shamans howled away ghosts responsible for disease. [29] Soul concept. [30] Spirits of departed humans do not return or communicate. [31] Stratagems to hoodwink. [32] Superghost doctrine not yet wholly rejected. [33] Superstition of ghosts dream still persists. [34] Supposed human origin of ghosts gods. [35]
