Caiaphas

See also: UB 186:1.1; UB 190:3.3.

Annas’s aggressive son-in-law. [1]High priest. [2]Asserted it better one die than community perish. [3]Hardhearted and vainglorious. [4]Accused Jesus before Pilate. [5]Judas met with Jesus’ enemies at home of. [6]Temple guards ordered Jesus taken to. [7]Sanhedrin met at palace of. [8]More prosecutor than unbiased judge. [9]Shook accusing finger at, smote Master in face. [10]Shook avenging finger in Pilate’s face. [11]
