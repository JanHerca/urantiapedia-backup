Achieving happiness

Depends upon willingness to be led by Adjuster. [1] Faith augments happiness. [2] Follows pure thinking, virtuous living. [3] Indissolubly linked with spiritual progress. [4] Linked with intelligent and enthusiastic pursuit of worthy goals. [5] None without intelligent effort. [6] Only by reconciling ego desire and altruistic urge. [7] Reward of deeds well done. [8]

Jesus was consistently cheerful. [9]

Nature of happiness

Being responsive to human need. [10] Can be acted out, can be lived. [11] Contentment is greatest wealth. [12] Does good like medicine. [13] Experience of those certain of God. [14] Integration of truth, beauty, and goodness. [15] Lesson regarding contentment. [16] Little dependent on environment. [17] Opportunity to pursue self-maintenance, self-perpetuation, and self-gratification. [18] Some persons naturally experience more. [19] Takes origin in inner life. [20] To child, happiness is immediate pleasure craving; too often associated with wealth. [21]
