Andrew

See also: UB 139:1; UB 143:3.1,5; UB 144:1.4; UB 145:2.1; UB 167:0.1; UB 191:0.6; UB 192:1.11; UB 192:4.7; UB 194:1.2.

First apostle of Jesus, chief of the group of apostles during Jesus life. Age 33 in A.D. 26. [1]Tendency to suspicion and over-individualism. [2]

Family

3 sisters, one named Martha; brother Peter. [3]Unmarried fisherman partner of Peter, James, and John. [4]

Work as apostle

Believer in John the Baptist; first apostle. [5]Deep-thinker; seasoned, philosophic counsel. [6]Selected Matthew. [7]Director general of apostles; efficient administrator; ablest apostle. [8]Chose Peter, James, and John to attend Jesus. [9]Sought Jesus after healing at sundown. [10]Defended rubbing grain on Sabbath. [11]Passed upon applicants for school of evangelists and the 70. [12]Imposed strict rules upon apostles’ work with women. [13]Opposed proclaiming Jesus king. [14]Attempted to heal James of Safed’s son. [15]Bewildered by entry into Jerusalem. [16]Handed sword back to Simon Zelotes. [17]Worried about Judas. [18]

Jesus

Questions. [19]Admonitions to Andrew. [20]Asked Andrew to be more trusting. [21]Asked Andrew to strengthen brethren. [22]

After Jesus death

Released from apostolic responsibility; silent. [23]Intimated Peter had seen things not real. [24]Fetched Simon Zelotes. [25]At Jesus’ appearances. [26]Never jealous of Peter. [27]Brought thousands into kingdom; crucified in Patrae. [28]

Gospel of Andrew

Partially recorded life of Jesus; made no copies; used in Part IV. [29]Luke had mutilated copy of A.’s notes. [30]
