Gautama Siddhartha

See also: UB 94:7.

Master and son of a chieftain ruling a mountain valley. Sudden conversion. [1]Doctrine of Teacher, Law, and Brotherhood. [2]

Followers

Gautama forbade to receive money. [3]Perverted cult to make him divine. [4]Wife founded order of nuns. [5]Son became successor, greatly extended cult. [6]Spiritual awakening of Gautama Siddhartha did not persist in India. [7]

Strengths

Brought succor to millions of despairing souls. [8]His life was much greater than his preachment. [9]Outstanding religious teacher. [10]Taught best godless philosophy ever invented. [11]Taught universal salvation, free from sacrifice, ritual, and priests. [12]

Weaknesses

Failed to perceive personality of God, existence of souls. [13]Faulty navigation charts ran aground. [14]Hardly believed in immortality. [15]Orphan prophet; knew God in spirit, not mind. [16]Should have heeded the hermit Godad. [17]
