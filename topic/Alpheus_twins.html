Alpheus twins

See also: UB 143:3.5.

James and Judas Alpheus or Alpheus twins were two apostles of Jesus. Mother Mary, father Alpheus. [1]

James Alpheus

Fisherman, farmer of Kheresa, selected by James. [2]Married with 3 children; also called Thaddeus. [3]

Judas Alpheus

Fisherman of Kheresa, selected by John Zebedee. [4]Married with 2 children; also called Lebbeus. [5]Questions of. [6]

Time with Jesus

Call of. [7]Assigned to manage the multitudes. [8]Fishermen from Kheresa; age 26 in A.D. 26. [9]Good-natured, simple-minded; servants of apostles. [10]Asleep by the fire. [11]Blissfully ignorant of controversy. [12]Attempted to dissuade Norana. [13]Put cloaks on Palm Sunday donkey; returned donkey to owner. [14]Enjoyed entry into Jerusalem. [15]Jesus’ admonitions to. After Jesus death

Believed on resurrection Sunday. [17]At Jesus’ appearances. [18]Jesus told to work as for God. [19]Returned to homes soon after Pentecost. [20]
