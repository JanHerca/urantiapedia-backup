Jesus stated that a true family is founded on the following seven facts: [1]

  1. The fact of existence. The children take origin in the parents.
  2. Security and pleasure. True fathers take great pleasure in providing for the needs of their children.
  3. Education and training. Wise fathers carefully plan for the education.
  4. Discipline and restraint. Farseeing fathers also make provision for the necessary discipline.
  5. Companionship and loyalty. The affectionate father holds intimate and loving intercourse with his children.
  6. Love and mercy. A compassionate father is freely forgiving.
  7. Provision for the future. Temporal fathers like to leave an inheritance for their sons.

We must call no man father in spiritual sense. [2] Children need fathers as well as mothers, and fathers need this parental experience as much as do mothers. [3]

The child must derive his first impressions of the universe from the mother’s care; he is wholly dependent on the earthly father for his first ideas of the heavenly Father. [4] Disciples must love gospel more than his father or mother. [5] The earliest races gave little credit to the father, looking upon the child as coming altogether from the mother. [6] Become the head of a human family is a highly educational and disciplinary responsibility. [7] It is intended that mortals who start out from an inhabited world have the experience of recognizing seven fathers: [8]

  1. The biologic father — the father in the flesh.
  2. The father of the realm — the Planetary Adam.
  3. The father of the spheres — the System Sovereign.
  4. The Most High Father — the Constellation Father.
  5. The universe Father — the Creator Son.
  6. The super-Fathers — the Ancients of Days.
  7. The spirit or Havona Father — the Universal Father.

Fathers are not like judges, enemies, or creditors. Real families are built upon tolerance, patience, and forgiveness. [9]
